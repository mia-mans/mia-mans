All galaxies have satellite galaxies that orbit, fall into, and otherwise interact with their host galaxy. Our Milky Way galaxy has one satellite of note: the Large Magellanic Cloud, or LMC. The LMC is a massive satellite with interactions that impact the kinematic structure of the Milky Way.

While the expected effects are well understood, the existing observational data is not dense enough to accurately identify any perturbations. However, emerging asteroseismic methods provide a promising solution. The data from these methods are expected to be available in the coming years.
