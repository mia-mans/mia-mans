Problem

Planning social activities with friends is hard. Groups often struggle to come up with interesting and new ideas, find times that work with everyone busy schedules, and face decision paralysis on where to finally go given various opinions.

This leads to social inertia where plans are never finalized and people opt to stay home instead of attending events and exploring their area.

Goals

Create a simple app that inspires and facilitates the organization of social activies.

I. Spark inspiration - discover new eateries, hidden gems, and limited time events

II. Get organized - create an easy, systematic way to plan, share, and discuss ideas and make decisions with your group

III. Save your thoughts - have a private workspace to save ideas in your personal collections

I. Initial Research

User Interviews

I conducted a survey and multiple in depth one-on-one interviews with people from various age groups and geographical locations to learn more about how people interact with their friends and neighborhoods. From both the interviews and short form surveys, I was able to identify major themes regarding how people organize time with friends and what they choose to do together.

Research Synthesis

After synthesizing my findings, I came away with a list of insights. The most informative of these were as follows:

Need for Planning Efficiency

As someone with a busy schedule and friends with similarly busy schedules, I need to be able to efficiently and effectively plan times to go out.

Difficult to Remember Ideas

As someone who finds new restaurants and experiences from a variety of sources, I need a streamlined way to keep track of options and share them with friends.

Lacking Inspiration to Discover Places

As someone who feels that I've exhausted the surrounding options, I need a way to discover new and exciting things near me.

Hard to Find Limited Time Opportunities

As someone who enjoys limited-time events, I need to be able to discover these experiences in advance so that I can make plans.

How Might We Statements

After establishing the key interview insights, I formulated top problems to solve.

How might we introduce users to new and exciting things in their area?

How might we integrate a planning system that allows users to discuss their schedules within a group?

How might we design social outings with a focus on convenience in location?

How might we allow users to save and share restaurants and experiences with their friends?

III. High Fidelity Prototypes

With simplicity in mind, my final app includes a cohesive design across screens, easy to navigate buttons and sections, and a similar interface between mobile and desktop versions.

The Homepage

The app initially opens to a scrollable feed of nearby establishments and pop up events. Users are able to toggle between a list and map view, as well as click on each card to get additional details about the location.

Trip Planning

Users are able to collaboratively plan trips with other users. The app shows the expected time duration and proximity of the locations to streamline the planning process. Users can also chat with each other within the trip and easily send additional locations back and forth without committing to adding them to the schedule.

Collections

Users can save establishments to their own private collections. This makes it simple for users to access their bucket-list locations when planning with friends.

Final Reflections

Overall, I was happy with the outcome of this design project. I feel that I successfully created an app to allow people to connect with friends and their cities in an accessible and convenient way. My understanding of design principles improved significantly throughout the process, and I'm very happy with the simplistic and approachable design of the app.

• Prototyping and micro-interactions: I would like to have spent more time prototyping all of the interactions within the app, such as small animations when adding locations to a list or options to navigate within the map view.

• Further design development: Although I'm very happy with the simplistic design of this app, with more time I would have developed more exciting design decisions. For example, I could imagine a more engaging card for pop-up events and/or more exciting options within the chat feature.
